# DEC-ADWM-004: FUSE cycle 2 for EX-1-008; escalate DHC gold reference to external-Gate

## Decision summary

Cycle 1 of EX-1-008 landed commit `60952b6` with a correct mean-over-y
wall-gradient algorithm (CHK-1,2,4,5,6,9,10 PASS). The binding physics
gate CHK-3 subsequently returned **Nu = 77.82** on the B1 wall-packed
256² mesh — outside the target band [25, 35] and above the pre-refactor
value of 66.25. Per `fix_plan_packet.md §4 COND-3` this triggers REJECT
of cycle 1.

**opus47-main FUSES cycle 2** (does NOT dispatch Option B snGrad
patch integration) and instead escalates the finding to external-Gate
for gold-reference accuracy review.

## Honest finding

The refactor is algorithmically correct. The mean-over-y of wall-adjacent
gradients is the right quantity to compute, and its collapse on
y-invariant synthetic data reproduces the existing test's Nu=10.5.

The B1 measurement Nu ≈ 77.8 is closer to the physically expected
mean-Nu at Ra=1e10 than the gold_standard's stated `ref_value: 30.0`
appears to be:

| source | Nu_mean at Ra ≈ 1e10 | notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dhir 2001 (gold_standard source citation) | ~30 (claimed in ref_value) | but gold's own reference_correlation_context acknowledges "500-1000 wall-normal cells" requirement and `satisfied_by_current_adapter: false` |
| Fusegi et al. 1991 (3D DHC, Ra=1e10) | ~140 | different geometry |
| Ampofo & Karayiannis 2003 (2D air, Ra=1.58e9) | ~62 | Ra one decade lower |
| Mesh-scaling extrapolation (Nu ~ Ra^0.28) | ~140–180 | coarse estimate |

At Ra=1e10 on a 256² mesh that is still under-resolving the δ/L ≈ 3.2e-3
thermal BL (precondition #1 explicitly `satisfied_by_current_adapter:
false`), Nu ~ 78 is a credible intermediate measurement. A fully
resolved 1000-cell wall-normal run would likely return a higher number,
closer to 140-160.

**The gold `ref_value: 30.0` appears to disagree with both literature
for Ra=1e10 and with the refined measurement. This is a gold-reference
accuracy question that only external-Gate (or Kogami directly) can
rule on.**

## Why not cycle 2 (Option B snGrad patch integration)?

Option B replaces the between-first-two-cells gradient with a
face-integrated `snGrad(T)` over the hot patch, which is strictly a
MORE accurate approximation of the true wall heat flux. On a
well-posed physical setup, Option B should give a value at or above
Option C's mean (since wall-adjacent gradient is a downward-biased
proxy). Option B is therefore expected to land at ≥ 78, possibly
closer to 140-160 as mesh refines toward full BL resolution.

Running cycle 2 would consume Codex quota and solver wall-clock to
confirm what is already implied by the theory: the solver is producing
values CLOSER to the physically expected literature value, and
progressively FURTHER from the gold_standard's stated ref_value=30.
This is a waste of cycles and does not close the real open question,
which is a gold-accuracy question.

are external-Gate decisions.
